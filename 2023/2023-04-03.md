* Found out the hard way not to use angle cutters underneath header pins to remove them. It‚Äôs been scratching the soldermask on my boards, did it again last night and noticed it immediately. The cutter nicks the soldermask and exposes the copper ground plane next to the pin holes. 
*   * Whatever pin the scratch is next can get soldered straight to the exposed ground plane. This is especially bad if it happens next to the 3V3 pin (which I did). Amazingly the board will still function to an extent. Yes, I had a moment and plugged it in like that intentionally, for science. It causes every GPIO to get locked HIGH because there‚Äôs no more ground plane. Thankfully I can say this doesn‚Äôt cause any electrical damage and is fixable with nail polish (or conformal coating) and careful soldering. From now on I‚Äôll be snipping pins in the middle away from the PCB.
